# The Story Behind the Hypersonic Defense Screener

*A work in progress — updated as the project evolves.*

---

## Origins

I spent 21 years in the United States Army — enlisted Signal Corps 
as a network operator and analyst, then commissioned as an Infantry 
officer and later Civil Affairs. After college in Indiana and a short 
career as an Assembly language programmer at an insurance company in 
Michigan, I joined the Army in 1999. After retirement as a Major, 
I earned a masters in cybersecurity, worked a year for the FBI, 
and then joined protecting critical technical defense programs.

The investment thesis didn't start with a research report. It started 
with a hobby. In college in Indiana, I used to calculate when and where 
Iridium satellites would be visible in the night sky — tracking the 
constellation pass by pass. Years later I realized I could own a piece 
of it. That's when the picks-and-shovels idea took hold.

If the people building the next generation of weapons systems need 
titanium alloys, advanced composites, hypersonic test platforms, and 
AI command software — why not own the companies supplying those things? 
Not the primes everyone knows. The layer beneath them.

This screener is my attempt to systematize that instinct.

---

## The Build

The project started with a simple question: what if I could apply 
the same discipline I used in the Army — clear objectives, documented 
processes, measurable outcomes — to investing?

The answer was a rules-based screener built from scratch. Not a 
copy of someone else's system. Not a black box. Every decision 
documented, every signal explainable, every limitation honestly stated.

We started with the thesis. Then the universe. Then the code.
In that order. Always in that order.

### Phase 1 — Foundation (June 2026)
- Defined the investment thesis: picks-and-shovels play on the 
  hypersonic and next-generation defense supply chain
- Built a 13-ticker universe across four technology domains
- Wrote a rules-based screener with technical, fundamental, 
  and thesis scoring
- Backtested against 5 years of historical data
- Deployed a live dashboard accessible from iPhone

### Phase 2 — Intelligence (June 2026)
- Added automated monitoring of Pentagon contracts, 
  Congressional defense legislation, and SEC IPO filings
- Built a discovery scanner to continuously surface 
  new universe candidates from defense ETFs and SEC filings
- Switched to Tradier API for cloud-reliable price data

### Phase 3 — Execution (In Progress)
- Portfolio tracker — see the active lineup vs the full roster
- Robinhood Agentic integration — execute trades from signals
- Rebalancing engine — know when to rotate and by how much
- Signal history — track form over time, not just today

## The Philosophy

The universe is the roster. The active portfolio is the starting 
lineup. The screener is the coaching staff's analytics system.

Players rotate based on performance. The best players are always 
on the field. When form drops, they go to the bench. When a new 
player earns their spot, they come off the bench.

The goal is never to find the one perfect stock. The goal is to 
continuously keep the best companies on the field.

---

## The Road to Autopilot

If this system performs — and the signals prove reliable over time — 
the next step is publishing it as an Autopilot Pilot. Not because 
the returns are guaranteed, but because the process is transparent, 
the thesis is honest, and the methodology is documented from day one.

That documentation is this repository.
